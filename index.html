Point One

From Data to Meaning

There is a whole discipline in between.

Technology solved the first two steps (collection, integration). The translation from data to meaning - almost nobody is doing that.

03 / 22

Point One (cont)

Technology invested everything in the first two steps, left the last two empty

Stages 1-3: Prepare, Collect, IntegrateStages 4-5: Reflect, Act
Technology rich, apps do wellDesign blank, nobody doing it
Li, Dey & Forlizzi (CHI 2010): Existing tech invests heavily in first three stages, leaving the last two almost blank. Rooksby (2014): Real tracking full of discontinuity, forgetting, and restarting.

These two blanks are exactly the designer core value space.

04 / 22

Point Two

No Neutral Charts, Only Designers Choosing What to Show

Hullman & Diakopoulos (2011):
Every data visualization is not a mirror reflection but a rhetorical act filtered through choices.

05 / 22

Point Two (cont)

Applied to Sports Scenes

Loss Frame
  • Your error rate is 35%
  • You are 15 percent slower than average
  • Training days insufficient this month

User feels: I suck.

Gain Frame
  • Your success rate is 65 percent
  • You are at the 85th percentile
  • You have spent 12 days on court

User feels: I am improving.

Data has not changed. What changed is the way it is told. The way it is told - is design.

06 / 22

Point Three

The key to persistence is not skill but belief

Bandura (1977): Self-efficacy is the belief that one can successfully execute a behavior to produce a desired result.

NOT can you actually do it, but do you believe you can?

07 / 22

Point Three (cont)

The duality of Duolingo Streak

Positive
  • Retention from 12 to 55 percent
  • Emotional anchor, identity marker, social currency
  • Leverages loss aversion
Negative
  • Users choose simple questions to maintain streak
  • When streak breaks: total abandonment
  • Target shifts from learn well to do not break

External motivation can start behavior but long-term reliance erodes intrinsic motivation.

08 / 22

Point Three (cont)

Stagnation might be when the brain is busiest

Research shows after a plateau period usually comes a sudden jump in performance. Plateau followed by breakthrough.

Cognitive reframing strategy: When detecting a plateau, intervene: You are in a consolidation phase. Your brain is upgrading new skills.

Dayan and Katz (1992): Media events define 2011 perfectly. But 2024 does not fit anymore: the event itself only accounts for 9 percent. Personalized narratives account for 51 percent.

Algorithms have shattered collective ritual into countless micro-rituals.

10 / 22

Point Four (cont)

When Participation meaning changes

Bourdieu (1984): Preferences for sports are not taste but class habitus externalized.

Old money loves Tenniscore. You do not need to know how to play tennis - you just need to dress like someone who does to complete a class identity performance.

When we say 23.81 million tennis population - are we counting players, or skirt buyers?

11 / 22

Point Five

Data is not the truth of the body

It is a portrait of the body.

Merleau-Ponty (1945): AI cameras translate the subjective body into the objective body. Translation is necessarily lossy.

The body is the general media through which we possess a world.

12 / 22

Point Five (cont)

Let us stop pretending data is perfect

Giorgia Lupi (2017), Data Humanism: Two designers, one year, weekly hand-drawn postcards. Lines crooked. Colors uneven. This imperfection makes data intimate and warm.

Her first solo exhibition Data Humanism opens in New York April 30, 2026.

Perhaps the best sports data visualization is not the most accurate one but the most human one.

13 / 22

Point Six

Most successful personal data narrative product in the last 10 years

2024 Wrapped: 3.5 billion users, 1.4 billion reports, 2.3 billion social impressions.

  • Temporal Capsule: Activates autobiographical memory
  • Identity Construction: Share I am this kind of person
  • Social Currency: Every frame independently shareable
  • Gain Framing Extreme: Listened 300 times to same song - unique personality not OCD

14 / 22

Point Six (cont)

But is only saying nice things enough?

Negative Example

Strava puts the full report behind an 80 dollar paywall

Pure narrative without information depth also dissatisfies users.

Ethical Boundary

Do not tamper with data. Do not hide risks. Provide the deep look option.

Good design is like a good coach: honest when encouraging, direct when confronting, always with you can get better.

15 / 22

Point Seven

What you choose to slow down is saying this moment matters more

Flow State: Self-awareness disappears. Time perception distorts. The ball seems to hover in the air.

16 / 22

Point Eight

When everyone has professional level treatment, does cool still exist?

Walter Benjamin (1935): Aura - the unreachable feeling an artwork has because it is unique. Machine reproduction eliminates it. But we noticed an exception: worship value retreats to the last fortress.

When everyone looks equally cool, cool itself depreciates. Unless the aura comes from data uniqueness, not template scarcity.

17 / 22

Point Eight (cont)

When data goes from measurement tool to creative medium

GPS data records distance and pace. But Strava Art users turned it into a medium for self-expression. Non-reproducible: a specific person, in a specific city, using their body for hours.

That is a data portrait, not a data scorecard.

❓

Forgetting yourself and Being seen
Can they coexist?

The most precious moment of flow is forgetting yourself - completely immersed in the rhythm of hitting the ball. But every design discussed today is about being seen.

Perhaps the best design is:
Make the cameras presence light enough to be forgotten,
make the generated work deep enough to be worthy of being gazed at.
